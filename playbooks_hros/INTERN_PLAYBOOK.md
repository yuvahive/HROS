## 1. Getting Started

### Welcome to HROS!

As an intern, you have access to HROS to help you during your internship. This playbook will guide you through everything you need to know.

### Login

1. Navigate to the HROS application URL
2. Enter your email and password (provided by your manager or HR)
3. You'll land on **My Dashboard**

### First-Time Setup

1. **Change your password** — Go to My Profile and update it
2. **Complete your profile** — Add your skills and preferences
3. **Explore the dashboard** — See what's available
4. **Ask your manager** about any questions

### Your Access Overview

As an Intern, you have access to **8 boards**:

| Category | Your Access |
|----------|-------------|
| MY SPACE | My Dashboard, My Profile, Organization, Work Uploads |
| TEAM | Team Sync, Team Work, 1:1 Meetings, Action Items |

### What You Can Do

| Feature | Your Access |
|---------|-------------|
| View your own data | Full |
| View team activity | View only |
| Upload work | Create, Read |
| View tasks | Read only |
| View org chart | View only |
| View action items | Read only |

## 2. Board-by-Board Deep Dive

### MY SPACE — Personal Boards

---

#### My Dashboard

**What it is:**
Your starting point each day. Shows your tasks, team activity, and upcoming meetings.

**Why it exists:**
Gives you a quick overview of what's happening without navigating multiple boards. It helps you prioritize your day and stay on top of your work.

**When to use it:**
- Every morning when you start work
- After returning from a break
- When you need a quick status check

**What good looks like:**
- You check it first thing every morning
- You know what tasks are assigned to you
- You can see what your team is working on
- You're prepared for your 1:1 meetings

**As an Intern, you specifically see:**
- Tasks assigned to you
- Team activity from your team
- Upcoming 1:1 meetings with your mentor

**Daily intern routine:**
1. **Morning:** Check dashboard for new tasks
2. **During day:** Update Team Sync with your progress
3. **Before 1:1:** Prepare questions and updates
4. **End of day:** Log your work

**Best practices:**
- Check it first thing every morning
- Don't ignore assigned tasks
- Use it to prepare for your day

---

#### My Profile

**What it is:**
Your personal identity card within HROS. Stores your name, email, role (Intern), team, skills, and preferences.

**Why it exists:**
Centralizes your professional identity. Your manager and HR can see your skills and assign appropriate tasks based on your profile.

**When to use it:**
- When you first set up your account
- When you gain new skills or complete courses
- When you want to update your preferences

**What good looks like:**
- Complete profile with all fields filled
- Skills list updated as you learn new things
- Preferences reflecting your work style

**Updating your profile:**
1. Click **"Edit Profile"**
2. Add your skills:
   - What technologies you know
   - What you're learning
   - Certifications or courses completed
3. Update your preferences
4. Click **Save**

**Why update your profile?**
- Helps your manager understand your skills
- Helps assign appropriate tasks
- Tracks your growth during the internship
- Useful for future reference (recommendations, job applications)

**Best practices:**
- Keep your skills list current
- Add certifications as you earn them
- Update preferences if your situation changes
- Be honest about your skill levels

#### Organization (Org Chart)

**What it is:**
A visual map of your entire company's reporting structure. Shows who reports to whom, team compositions, and role distributions.

**Why it exists:**
Helps you understand where you fit in the organization. You can see your team, find your manager and mentor, and understand the company structure.

**When to use it:**
- When you first join (understand the structure)
- When you need to find someone
- When you want to learn about different teams
- When you're curious about the organization

**What good looks like:**
- You can find your team and colleagues easily
- You understand the reporting structure
- You know who to contact for different things

**How to navigate:**
1. **Zoom** — Scroll to zoom in/out
2. **Pan** — Click and drag to move around
3. **Click** — Click a person to see their name and role
4. **Search** — Find specific people

**Finding your team:**
1. Search for your team name
2. See who's on your team
3. Find your manager and mentor
4. Understand the team structure

**Best practices:**
- Explore the org chart to understand the company
- Use the search bar to find specific people
- Click on people to learn about their roles
- Understand your reporting lines

#### Work Uploads

**What it is:**
A centralized workspace for sharing your deliverables with your mentor and team. Creates a structured review workflow: Uploaded → Under Review → Approved/Needs Revision/Rejected.

**Why it exists:**
This is how you show your work and get feedback. Instead of sending files over chat or email, you upload them here for formal review. Your mentor reviews your work, provides feedback, and rates your output.

**When to use it:**
- When you've completed a deliverable
- When you want feedback on your work
- When you want to showcase what you've learned
- When your mentor asks you to submit something

**What good looks like:**
- Clear titles that describe the work
- Descriptions explaining what you learned
- Correct categorization
- Complete, polished work

**Creating an upload:**
1. Click **"+ Add Upload"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Title** (required) — Name of your work
   - **Description** — What it is and what you learned
   - **Category** — Document, Code, Design, or Presentation
   - **File/Data** — Attach your work
3. Click **Save**

**What happens next:**
1. Your mentor reviews your submission
2. They provide feedback and a rating
3. You'll be notified of the result
4. Use the feedback to improve

**Learning from feedback:**

| Status | What to Do |
|--------|-----------|
| **Approved** | Great job! Your work met expectations. Keep it up. |
| **Needs Revision** | Read feedback carefully. Make changes. Upload revised version. Ask questions if unsure. |
| **Rejected** | Don't worry — this is a learning opportunity. Read feedback thoroughly. Discuss with mentor. Try again. |

**Tips for good uploads:**
- **Show your learning** — Describe what you learned, not just what you did
- **Be thorough** — Include all relevant details
- **Ask for feedback** — Let your mentor know what you want input on
- **Complete work** — Upload finished pieces, not partial work
- **Use correct categories** — Helps your mentor find and assess your work

**Best practices:**
- Upload regularly — don't wait until the end of your internship
- Include a reflection on what you learned
- Respond to feedback promptly
- Ask questions if you're unsure about feedback
- Use the category system for searchability

### TEAM — Collaboration Boards

#### Team Sync

**What it is:**
A real-time standup replacement. See what everyone on your team is working on, and share what you're working on — without scheduling a meeting.

**Why it exists:**
Replaces daily standups with an async update system. You post what you're working on, your status, and when you last updated. Your manager and team can see at a glance who's on track, who's blocked, and who needs help.

**When to use it:**
- Start of day: Post what you plan to work on
- Mid-day: Update progress if significant
- End of day: Post what you accomplished
- Immediately: If you hit a blocker

**What good looks like:**
- You update at least once daily
- "Blocked" statuses are resolved quickly
- Notes provide enough context
- You check teammates' updates

**Adding your update:**
1. Click **"+ Add Update"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Working On** — What you're currently focused on
   - **Status** — On Track / Blocked / Needs Help
   - **Notes** — What you've accomplished or need help with
3. Click **Save**

**When to update:**

| When | What to Post |
|------|-------------|
| **Start of day** | What you plan to work on |
| **When blocked** | Immediately update status |
| **End of day** | What you accomplished |

**Status guide:**

| Status | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|
| **On Track** | Making good progress |
| **Blocked** | Something is preventing your progress |
| **Needs Help** | You need guidance or assistance |

**Why this matters:**
- Keeps your mentor informed without constant check-ins
- Shows your progress and professionalism
- Helps identify blockers early
- Demonstrates good communication skills

**Best practices:**
- Update at least once daily
- Be specific — "working on API integration" is better than "coding"
- Change status immediately when unblocked
- Check for teammates' blocked statuses
- Use notes to provide context

#### Team Work (Kanban)

**What it is:**
A visual task management board. Tasks move through columns: To Do → In Progress → Done.

**Why it exists:**
Shows you what work is assigned to you and your team. You can see what's queued, what's being worked on, and what's finished.

**When to use it:**
- When you want to see your assigned tasks
- When you want to understand what your team is working on
- When your manager assigns you new work

**What good looks like:**
- You can find your tasks easily
- You understand what each task requires
- You know the priority and deadline

**What you can do:**
- **View** all tasks on the board
- **Read** task details
- **See** who's assigned to what

**What you cannot do:**
- Create new tasks (your mentor assigns them)
- Edit tasks (only your mentor/manager can)
- Move tasks between columns

**Finding your tasks:**
1. Look for tasks assigned to you
2. Check the **To Do** column for new work
3. Check **In Progress** for ongoing work
4. Check **Done** for completed tasks

**Understanding task cards:**

Each card shows:
- **Title** — What the task is
- **Assignee** — Who's responsible
- **Priority** — How important (Low/Medium/High/Critical)
- **Due Date** — When it's needed

**Best practices:**
- Check the board daily for new tasks
- Understand the priority before starting
- Read task descriptions carefully
- If you're unsure about a task, ask your mentor
- Don't start work without understanding the requirements

#### 1:1 Meetings

**What it is:**
Regular meetings with your mentor or manager to discuss your progress, get feedback, and plan your next steps.

**Why it exists:**
This is your most important recurring meeting. It's where you get feedback, ask questions, discuss your growth, and plan your internship. Don't waste it by showing up unprepared.

**When to use it:**
- Weekly (or as scheduled)
- Before the meeting: Prepare topics
- During the meeting: Listen, discuss, take notes
- After the meeting: Follow up on commitments

**What good looks like:**
- You come prepared with topics and questions
- You listen actively to feedback
- You take notes on action items
- You follow through on commitments
- You bring up concerns before they become problems

**Preparing for your 1:1:**
1. Open the 1:1 card
2. Add **Prep Notes** — What you want to discuss:
   - Questions you have
   - Work you want feedback on
   - Things you've learned
   - Challenges you're facing
   - Career interests and goals
3. Review your recent work uploads
4. Think about your goals

**During the meeting:**
1. Open the 1:1 card
2. Go through your prep notes
3. Listen to feedback
4. Take notes on action items
5. Ask clarifying questions

**After the meeting:**
1. Review the **Summary** — Key takeaways
2. Check **Action Items** — What you need to do
3. Follow through on commitments
4. Note topics for next meeting

**Good topics for intern 1:1s:**
- Progress on assigned tasks
- Skills you're developing
- Questions about the company or industry
- Feedback on your work
- Career interests and goals
- Challenges or blockers
- What you've learned
- What you want to learn next
- How you're feeling about the internship

**Best practices:**
- Come prepared — don't improvise
- Be honest about challenges
- Ask questions — no question is too small
- Follow up on previous action items
- Use the meeting for growth, not just status updates
- Don't cancel unless absolutely necessary
- Take notes — you'll forget details otherwise

#### Action Items

**What it is:**
A board showing tasks and decisions assigned to you from meetings, 1:1s, and other sources.

**Why it exists:**
Tracks your commitments. When your mentor assigns you something or you volunteer for a task, it goes here. It answers: "What did I agree to do, and when is it due?"

**When to use it:**
- When your mentor assigns you something
- After meetings where you volunteered for something
- When you need to track a commitment

**What good looks like:**
- You check your action items regularly
- You understand what each item requires
- You know the deadline
- You communicate if you're blocked

**What you can do:**
- **View** your action items
- **Read** the details
- **See** the status and deadline

**What you cannot do:**
- Create action items (your mentor/manager creates them)
- Edit action items
- Change the status

**Action item status:**

| Status | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Assigned** | Given to you, not yet started |
| **In Progress** | You're working on it |
| **Review** | Done, waiting for verification |
| **Complete** | Finished and verified |

**Best practices:**
- Check your action items daily
- Start work promptly
- Communicate if you're blocked or need more time
- Complete items by the due date
- Ask questions if you're unsure about requirements

## 5. Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Can't see a board | Your role has limited access; this is normal |
| Can't create a task | Interns can view tasks but not create them |
| Can't edit action items | You can view but not edit; your manager manages these |
| Upload not appearing | Check it was saved; contact your mentor |
| Data not updating | Check internet connection; data syncs every 3 seconds |

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why can't I see HR Operations?**
A: HR Operations (hiring, onboarding, etc.) are restricted to HR and admin roles. This is normal.

**Q: Can I create tasks for myself?**
A: No, only your manager or mentor can create tasks. You can view and update your status on assigned tasks.

**Q: Can I see other interns' profiles?**
A: You can see basic info on the org chart. Detailed profiles are restricted.

**Q: How do I get more access?**
A: Talk to your manager about expanding your responsibilities. They can discuss role changes with HR.

**Q: Can I view reports?**
A: No, reports are restricted to HR and manager roles.

**Q: How do I contact HR?**
A: Use your 1:1 meeting with your mentor, or reach out via your team's communication channel.

**Q: What if I need help?**
A: Update your Team Sync status to "Needs Help" or "Blocked." Your mentor will see it and reach out.

**Q: Can I view my internship details?**
A: Your manager tracks your internship progress. Ask them about your goals and evaluations.

**Q: How do I upload my work?**
A: Go to Work Uploads > + Add Upload. Fill in the title, description, category, and attach your file.

**Q: What if I don't understand a task?**
A: Ask your mentor before starting. It's better to clarify upfront than to do work incorrectly.
