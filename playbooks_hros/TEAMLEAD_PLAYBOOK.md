## 1. Getting Started

### Login

1. Navigate to the HROS application URL
2. Enter your email and password
3. You'll land on **My Dashboard**

### Your Access Overview

As a TeamLead, you have access to **17 boards**. Your data is scoped to your assigned team.

| Category | Your Access |
|----------|-------------|
| MY SPACE | My Dashboard, My Profile, Organization, Work Uploads |
| TEAM | Team Sync, Team Work, 1:1 Meetings, Projects, Action Items |
| HR OPERATIONS | Hiring Pipeline, Onboarding, Internships, Team Pulse, Exits & Alumni (read-only) |
| TEAMS | Teams |
| WELLBEING | Team Wellness |
| INSIGHTS | Metrics (read-only), Reports (read + export) |

### Data Scoping

Your data is filtered by `filterByTeam()` — you only see:
- Projects owned by your team
- Tasks assigned to your team members
- Action items assigned to your team
- Team members in your assigned team

## 2. Board-by-Board Deep Dive

### MY SPACE — Personal Boards

---

#### My Dashboard

**What it is:**
Your personal command center. Aggregates everything that needs your attention into a single view.

**Why it exists:**
As a manager, you juggle multiple responsibilities — people management, project oversight, hiring, and your own deliverables. This board surfaces what needs your attention without requiring you to check 5+ boards.

**When to use it:**
- Every morning when you start work
- After returning from a meeting
- Before leaving for the day

**What good looks like:**
- You scan the dashboard and know what needs attention
- No pending tasks are overdue
- Team activity is visible at a glance

**As a TeamLead, you specifically see:**
- Your team's task status
- Pending reviews (work uploads from your team)
- Upcoming 1:1s
- Action items assigned to you

**Best practices:**
- Check it first thing every morning
- Use it to prepare for your day
- Don't ignore overdue items

---

#### My Profile

**What it is:**
Your personal identity card within HROS. Stores your name, email, role, team, skills, preferences, and compensation history.

**Why it exists:**
Centralizes your professional identity. Your team members can see your role and contact info on the org chart.

**When to use it:**
- When you first set up your account
- When you gain new skills
- When reviewing your compensation history

**Best practices:**
- Keep your profile complete
- Update skills regularly
- Review compensation history before salary discussions

---

#### Organization (Org Chart)

**What it is:**
A visual map of your entire company's reporting structure.

**Why it exists:**
Shows you where your team fits in the larger organization. Helps you understand cross-team dependencies and find the right people for collaboration.

**When to use it:**
- When you need to find someone's manager
- When planning cross-team projects
- When onboarding new team members
- When understanding the broader org structure

**Best practices:**
- Use the search bar to quickly find specific people
- Zoom out for the big picture
- Keep your team's reporting lines accurate

---

#### Work Uploads

**What it is:**
A centralized workspace for reviewing work submitted by your team members.

**Why it exists:**
Replaces scattered file shares and email attachments with a structured review workflow. Creates accountability and a paper trail for all deliverables.

**When to use it:**
- When reviewing your team's submissions
- When you need to submit your own work for review
- When tracking review status

**What good looks like:**
- Reviews happen within 24-48 hours
- Feedback is specific and actionable
- Status is updated promptly

**Best practices:**
- Review uploads promptly — your team is waiting for feedback
- Provide constructive feedback, not just "approved"
- Use the rating system consistently
- Acknowledge good work publicly

### TEAM — Collaboration Boards

---

#### Team Sync

**What it is:**
A real-time standup replacement. See what everyone on your team is working on without scheduling a meeting.

**Why it exists:**
This is your most important daily tool. It replaces daily standups with an async update system. You can see at a glance who's on track, who's blocked, and who needs help — without scheduling a single meeting.

**When to use it:**
- Every morning: Check for overnight updates
- Multiple times daily: Monitor for "Blocked" statuses
- End of day: See what your team accomplished

**What good looks like:**
- Everyone on your team updates at least once daily
- "Blocked" statuses get resolved within hours
- Notes provide enough context that you don't need to ask clarifying questions
- You can scan the board in 30 seconds and know your team's pulse

**As a TeamLead, you specifically can:**
- See all team member updates
- Respond to blocked statuses
- Help unblock team members
- Monitor update frequency

**Best practices:**
- Check for "Blocked" items first thing each morning
- Respond to blockers immediately
- Encourage your team to update daily
- Use notes to provide context for async communication
- Don't let "Blocked" statuses sit for more than a few hours

---

#### Team Work (Kanban)

**What it is:**
A visual task management board. Tasks move through columns: To Do → In Progress → Done.

**Why it exists:**
This is where you manage your team's workload. It shows what's being worked on, what's queued, and what's finished. It prevents work from falling through the cracks and makes bottlenecks visible.

**When to use it:**
- When assigning tasks to team members
- When checking on task progress
- When prioritizing work
- During sprint planning

**What good looks like:**
- Tasks are small and focused (1-3 days each)
- "In Progress" column isn't overloaded (3-5 tasks per person max)
- "Done" column gets cleared regularly
- Each task has clear acceptance criteria

**As a TeamLead, you specifically can:**
- Create tasks for your team members
- Move tasks between columns
- Delete tasks
- Assign priorities and due dates

**Best practices:**
- Keep tasks small — break large work into smaller pieces
- Assign a single owner to each task
- Set realistic due dates
- Don't leave tasks in "In Progress" for more than 3 days
- Use priority levels consistently (Critical/High/Medium/Low)
- Link tasks to relevant projects

---

#### 1:1 Meetings

**What it is:**
The most important recurring meeting in your management toolkit. Structured meeting scheduler and agenda manager for one-on-one meetings with your direct reports.

**Why it exists:**
1:1 meetings are where real management happens. This board ensures they actually happen, are well-prepared, and produce actionable outcomes. It tracks prep notes, discussion topics, and follow-up items.

**When to use it:**
- Weekly with each direct report (non-negotiable)
- Before the meeting: Review prep notes
- During the meeting: Take notes, record action items
- After the meeting: Summarize key decisions

**What good looks like:**
- Meetings happen on schedule (weekly)
- Prep notes are written before the meeting
- Action items have owners and deadlines
- Meeting summaries capture key decisions
- Follow-up items are tracked to completion

**As a TeamLead, you specifically can:**
- Schedule 1:1s with your team members
- Add prep notes and agenda items
- Take meeting notes
- Create action items from decisions
- Track meeting sentiment and quality

**1:1 Categories:**

| Category | What to Discuss |
|----------|----------------|
| **Shipping** | Tasks, deliverables, blockers, progress |
| **Growth** | Career goals, learning, feedback |
| **Wellbeing** | Workload, stress, work-life balance |

**Best practices:**
- Hold 1:1s weekly — consistency matters more than duration
- Let your direct report set the agenda
- Focus on their needs, not just status updates
- Follow up on previous action items before starting new topics
- Keep notes for continuity between meetings
- Don't cancel 1:1s unless absolutely necessary
- Use the meeting to coach, not just to status-check

---

#### Projects

**What it is:**
A health-tracking board for all active projects. Shows status, progress percentage, blockers, risk levels, and ownership.

**Why it exists:**
Projects fail silently when no one is tracking them. This board makes project health visible. It answers: "Is this project on track?" at a glance.

**When to use it:**
- When starting a new project
- Weekly: Update completion percentage
- When blockers arise: Flag them immediately
- When risk level changes: Update and escalate

**What good looks like:**
- Completion percentage is updated weekly
- Status accurately reflects reality
- Blockers are specific and have owners
- Risk levels are honest

**As a TeamLead, you specifically can:**
- Create, edit, and delete projects
- Assign owners and set priorities
- Monitor completion and blockers
- Escalate risks to HR or admin

**Best practices:**
- Assign a single owner to each project
- Update completion % weekly at minimum
- When status is "At Risk," add a specific plan to fix it
- Close completed projects
- Link projects to relevant tasks on the Team Work board

---

#### Action Items

**What it is:**
A decision-tracking and accountability board. Records decisions made in 1:1s and meetings, assigns owners, sets deadlines, and tracks completion.

**Why it exists:**
Decisions without owners don't get done. This board turns every decision into a trackable action item. It creates accountability by making who-owes-what-when explicit.

**When to use it:**
- After any 1:1 where decisions were made
- When someone volunteers to do something
- When you need to follow up on a promise
- When creating goals for your team

**What good looks like:**
- Every action item has a single owner
- Due dates are realistic
- Status is updated regularly
- Completed items are cleared

**As a TeamLead, you specifically can:**
- Create action items for yourself and your team
- Track completion status
- Follow up on overdue items
- Monitor completion rates

**Best practices:**
- Assign ONE owner per action item
- Set realistic due dates
- Break large items into smaller tasks
- Check action items daily
- Follow up on overdue items immediately
- Use action items from 1:1s to track commitments

### HR OPERATIONS — People Lifecycle Boards

---

#### Hiring Pipeline

**What it is:**
A Kanban board tracking every candidate from application to hire. Stages: Application → Screening → Interview → Offered → Hired.

**Why it exists:**
As a TeamLead, you need to hire for your team. This board lets you track candidates, add interview notes, and collaborate with HR on hiring decisions.

**When to use it:**
- When a new candidate is added for your team's roles
- After conducting an interview
- When providing feedback on candidates
- When making hiring decisions

**What good looks like:**
- Interview notes are detailed and specific
- Screening scores are consistent
- You collaborate with HR on decisions
- Candidates don't sit in stages for more than 1 week

**As a TeamLead, you specifically can:**
- Create, read, and update candidates
- Add interview notes and screening scores
- Move candidates through stages
- Collaborate with HR on offers

**Best practices:**
- Provide detailed interview feedback (not just "good" or "bad")
- Use a consistent scorecard for interviews
- Don't keep candidates waiting — respond within 48 hours
- Collaborate with HR on final decisions

---

#### Onboarding

**What it is:**
A 30-day roadmap for new hires joining your team.

**Why it exists:**
Your new hire's first 30 days set the tone for their entire tenure. This board ensures they get a structured, welcoming experience.

**When to use it:**
- When a new hire joins your team
- Daily during their first week
- Weekly for the first month
- At 30 days (complete onboarding)

**What good looks like:**
- Checklist items are checked off promptly
- You add manager notes regularly
- The new hire feels welcomed and supported
- Onboarding is completed within 30 days

**As a TeamLead, you specifically can:**
- Create onboarding cards for your team's new hires
- Assign buddies/mentors
- Track checklist completion
- Add manager notes and feedback
- Conduct the 30-day review

**Best practices:**
- Welcome the new hire on Day 1 personally
- Schedule regular 1:1s during onboarding
- Assign a buddy before they start
- Don't skip the 30-day review
- Document blockers to improve the process

#### Team Pulse

**What it is:**
A real-time sentiment and mood tracker for your team.

**Why it exists:**
Early warning system for team dysfunction. Detects declining energy, low morale, and collaboration issues before they become crises.

**When to use it:**
- Weekly: Add a pulse check for your team
- When you notice mood changes
- After significant events
- When team performance dips

**What good looks like:**
- Pulse checks happen regularly
- Scores are honest
- Notes explain context
- Action items are created from concerning trends

**As a TeamLead, you specifically can:**
- Add pulse checks for your team
- Monitor your team's health trends
- Take action on warning signs
- Escalate concerns to HR

**Best practices:**
- Don't skip weeks — consistency matters
- Be honest about scores
- When energy is low, investigate workload
- Track trends over time

## 4. Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Can't see other teams' data | This is by design — you're scoped to your team |
| Can't delete a candidate | TeamLead role doesn't have delete permission on hiring |
| Board shows no data | Verify your team assignment in Admin Settings |
| 1:1 not appearing | Check the date/time and team member assignment |
| Task not showing | Verify it's assigned to your team member |

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why can't I see all projects?**
A: You only see projects owned by your team members. Contact HR or admin for org-wide view.

**Q: Can I assign someone from another team?**
A: No, you can only assign tasks to your team members.

**Q: How do I escalate an issue?**
A: Use Action Items to create a high-priority item and assign it to HR or admin.

**Q: Can I change my team assignment?**
A: Only admins can change team assignments. Contact admin.
