## 2. Board-by-Board Deep Dive

### MY SPACE — Personal Boards

---

#### My Dashboard

**What it is:**
Your personal command center. Aggregates everything that needs your attention into a single, scannable view.

**Why it exists:**
As HR, you deal with multiple processes simultaneously — hiring, onboarding, exits, wellness checks. Without a dashboard, you'd have to check 5+ boards individually every morning. This eliminates that by surfacing urgent items in one place.

**When to use it:**
- Every morning when you start work
- After returning from a break
- When you need a quick status check

**What good looks like:**
- You open HROS, glance at the dashboard, and immediately know what needs attention
- No pending tasks are overdue
- You use Quick Actions to log work without leaving the page

**As HR, you specifically see:**
- Pending hiring actions (candidates stuck in stages)
- Onboarding items needing attention
- Exit process items requiring follow-up
- Wellness alerts across the org

**Best practices:**
- Check it first thing every morning
- Use Quick Action buttons for common tasks
- Don't ignore the "Pending Tasks" section

---

#### My Profile

**What it is:**
Your personal identity card within HROS. Stores your name, email, role, team, skills, preferences, and compensation history.

**Why it exists:**
Centralizes your professional identity. When you're referenced in HR processes, your profile data flows automatically.

**When to use it:**
- When you first set up your account
- When you gain new skills or certifications
- When you want to review your compensation history

**What good looks like:**
- Complete profile with all fields filled
- Skills list updated regularly
- Preferences reflecting your current work style

**Best practices:**
- Keep your skills list current
- Review compensation history before salary discussions
- Update preferences if your work situation changes

---

#### Organization (Org Chart)

**What it is:**
A visual map of your entire company's reporting structure. Shows who reports to whom, team compositions, and role distributions.

**Why it exists:**
Provides transparency for the entire organization. As HR, you use this to understand the current org structure, plan reorgs, and ensure reporting lines are accurate.

**When to use it:**
- When planning organizational changes
- When onboarding new employees
- When resolving reporting confusion
- When preparing org charts for leadership

**What good looks like:**
- Every person is visible and correctly placed
- Reporting lines are accurate
- Team groupings are clear

**Best practices:**
- Keep team assignments updated when people move teams
- Use the search bar to quickly find specific people
- Zoom out for the big picture, zoom in for details
- Export org charts for leadership presentations

---

#### Work Uploads

**What it is:**
A centralized workspace for sharing, reviewing, and managing work deliverables. Structured review workflow: Uploaded → Under Review → Approved/Needs Revision/Rejected.

**Why it exists:**
As HR, you need to review training materials, policy documents, and employee submissions. This board centralizes all review activity with accountability and a paper trail.

**When to use it:**
- When reviewing employee-submitted work
- When you need to submit HR materials for approval
- When tracking policy document reviews

**What good looks like:**
- Reviews happen within 24-48 hours
- Feedback is specific and actionable
- Status is updated promptly

**Best practices:**
- Review uploads promptly — employees are waiting for feedback
- Provide constructive feedback, not just "approved" or "rejected"
- Use categories consistently for searchability

### TEAM — Collaboration Boards

---

#### Team Sync

**What it is:**
A real-time standup replacement. See what everyone on your team is working on without scheduling a meeting.

**Why it exists:**
HR teams often span multiple locations and schedules. Team Sync gives you the same information as a standup, asynchronously. It surfaces blockers instantly.

**When to use it:**
- Start of day: Post what you plan to work on
- Mid-day: Update progress if significant
- End of day: Post what you accomplished
- Immediately: If you hit a blocker

**What good looks like:**
- Everyone on the team updates daily
- "Blocked" statuses get resolved within hours
- Notes provide enough context without follow-up questions

**Best practices:**
- Update at least once daily
- Be specific in "Working On" — "reviewing 5 candidate resumes" is better than "hiring work"
- Check for teammates' blocked statuses and offer help

---

#### Team Work (Kanban)

**What it is:**
A visual task management board using Kanban methodology. Tasks move: To Do → In Progress → Done.

**Why it exists:**
HR has many parallel processes — hiring, onboarding, exits, compliance. This board tracks all HR tasks visually so nothing falls through the cracks.

**When to use it:**
- When you start working on something (drag to In Progress)
- When you finish something (drag to Done)
- When your manager assigns you new work

**What good looks like:**
- Tasks are small and focused
- "In Progress" column isn't overloaded
- "Done" column gets cleared regularly
- Each task has clear completion criteria

**Best practices:**
- Keep tasks small — break large work into smaller pieces
- Don't leave tasks in "In Progress" for more than 3 days
- Update task status daily
- Use priority levels consistently

---

#### 1:1 Meetings

**What it is:**
A structured meeting scheduler and agenda manager for one-on-one meetings.

**Why it exists:**
HR conducts many 1:1s — with hiring managers, with employees, with leadership. This board ensures they're well-prepared and produce actionable outcomes.

**When to use it:**
- Before any 1:1 meeting
- When preparing agendas
- When recording meeting outcomes

**What good looks like:**
- Prep notes are written before meetings
- Action items have owners and deadlines
- Meeting summaries capture key decisions

**Best practices:**
- Hold regular 1:1s with key stakeholders
- Let the other party set part of the agenda
- Always create action items for decisions made
- Follow up on previous action items

---

#### Projects

**What it is:**
A health-tracking board for all active projects. Shows status, progress, blockers, risk levels, and ownership.

**Why it exists:**
HR runs projects — policy updates, training programs, compliance initiatives. This board makes project health visible and surfaces blockers before they become crises.

**When to use it:**
- When starting a new HR project
- Weekly: Update completion percentage
- When blockers arise: Flag them immediately
- When risk level changes: Update and escalate

**What good looks like:**
- Completion percentage is updated weekly
- Status accurately reflects reality
- Blockers are specific and have owners
- Risk levels are honest

**Best practices:**
- Assign a single owner to each project
- Update completion % weekly at minimum
- When status is "At Risk," add a specific plan to fix it
- Close completed projects

---

#### Action Items

**What it is:**
A decision-tracking and accountability board. Records decisions, assigns owners, sets deadlines, and tracks completion.

**Why it exists:**
HR decisions often involve multiple stakeholders. This board turns every decision into a trackable item with a clear owner and deadline.

**When to use it:**
- After any meeting where HR decisions were made
- When someone volunteers to do something
- When you need to follow up on a promise

**What good looks like:**
- Every action item has a single owner
- Due dates are realistic
- Status is updated regularly
- Completed items are cleared

**Best practices:**
- Assign ONE owner per action item
- Set realistic due dates
- Break large items into smaller tasks
- Check action items daily

### HR OPERATIONS — People Lifecycle Boards

---

#### Hiring Pipeline

**What it is:**
A Kanban board tracking every candidate from application to hire. Stages: Application → Screening → Interview → Offered → Hired.

**Why it exists:**
This is your primary tool for managing the recruitment process. Without it, candidates fall through cracks, interview feedback gets lost, and offers get delayed. It creates a structured, visible hiring process.

**When to use it:**
- When a new candidate applies
- After screening a resume
- After conducting an interview
- When making an offer
- When a candidate is hired

**What good looks like:**
- Every candidate is in the correct stage
- Screening scores are filled in before advancing
- Interview notes are detailed and actionable
- Offer details include salary, deadline, and conditions
- No candidate sits in a stage for more than 1 week without movement

**As HR, you specifically can:**
- Create, read, and update candidates
- Move candidates through all stages
- Add screening scores and interview notes
- Collaborate with hiring managers

**Best practices:**
- Don't keep rejected candidates in the pipeline — archive or delete
- Add screening scores consistently (1-10 scale)
- Include specific interview feedback, not just "good" or "bad"
- Track candidate source to identify best recruiting channels
- Review the pipeline weekly to identify stuck candidates
- Set up a standard interview scorecard for consistency

---

#### Onboarding

**What it is:**
A 30-day roadmap for new hires. Tracks checklist items, milestones, and progress from Day 1 through completion.

**Why it exists:**
First impressions matter. A disorganized onboarding process makes new hires feel unwelcome and unprepared. This board ensures every new hire gets a consistent, thorough onboarding experience.

**When to use it:**
- When a new hire starts (create their onboarding card)
- Daily during their first week (check off items)
- Weekly for the first month (update progress)
- At 30 days (complete onboarding)

**What good looks like:**
- Checklist items are checked off promptly
- Manager notes are added regularly
- Blockers are flagged and resolved quickly
- Onboarding is completed within 30 days
- New hire rates their onboarding experience positively

**As HR, you specifically can:**
- Create onboarding cards for any new hire
- Assign and reassign buddies/mentors
- Monitor onboarding completion rates
- Follow up on blocked onboarding items

**Best practices:**
- Start onboarding before Day 1 (send welcome email, prepare equipment)
- Assign a buddy before the new hire starts
- Schedule the first 1:1 on Day 1
- Don't skip the 30-day review
- Document blockers so you can improve the process
- Survey new hires about their onboarding experience

---

#### Internships

**What it is:**
Manages the full intern lifecycle: onboarding, goal setting, evaluations, certificate generation, and FTE recommendations.

**Why it exists:**
Internships are structured learning experiences. This board ensures interns get proper mentorship, clear goals, regular feedback, and a meaningful evaluation at the end.

**When to use it:**
- When an intern starts
- When setting goals (first week)
- During evaluations (weekly or bi-weekly)
- When generating completion certificates
- When recommending FTE conversion

**What good looks like:**
- Goals are specific and measurable
- Evaluations happen on schedule
- FTE recommendations are data-driven
- Certificates are generated and sent

**Best practices:**
- Set goals in the first week
- Conduct evaluations regularly (not just at the end)
- Document strengths and areas for improvement
- Generate certificates promptly upon completion
- Make FTE recommendations based on evaluation data

---

#### Team Pulse

**What it is:**
A real-time sentiment and mood tracker for teams. Shows energy levels, collaboration scores, morale, and workload balance.

**Why it exists:**
Teams don't fail because of technical problems — they fail because of people problems. This board detects early warning signs of dysfunction before they become crises.

**When to use it:**
- Weekly: Add a pulse check for each team
- When you notice mood changes
- After significant events (reorgs, layoffs, wins)
- When team performance dips

**What good looks like:**
- Pulse checks happen regularly (weekly)
- Scores are honest (not always "5/5")
- Notes explain the context behind scores
- Action items are created from concerning trends

**Best practices:**
- Don't skip weeks — consistency matters
- Be honest about scores
- When energy is low, investigate workload distribution
- When morale is negative, schedule a team conversation
- Track trends over time, not just single data points
- Correlate pulse data with performance data

---

#### Exits & Alumni

**What it is:**
Manages employee departures: exit interviews, knowledge transfer, access revocation, and alumni network tracking.

**Why it exists:**
How you treat departing employees defines your employer brand. This board ensures departures are handled professionally, knowledge is transferred, and the door is left open for future rehires.

**When to use it:**
- When an employee gives notice
- During the notice period (track KT)
- On the employee's last day
- After departure (alumni tracking)

**What good looks like:**
- KT is completed before the last day
- Exit interview notes are detailed and honest
- Access is revoked on the last day
- Alumni network is maintained

**Best practices:**
- Start KT planning immediately (don't wait until the last week)
- Conduct exit interviews in a safe, private setting
- Be honest about why people leave — it's valuable data
- Keep alumni contact info updated for potential rehires
- Review exit data quarterly for patterns
- Track boomerang potential for future rehire decisions

### TEAMS

---

#### Teams

**What it is:**
The team management hub. Three views: Org Chart (visual hierarchy), Tasks (team-level kanban), and Members (card/list of team members).

**Why it exists:**
Teams are the fundamental unit of work. This board defines who's on which team, who leads each team, and what teams are working on.

**When to use it:**
- When creating a new team
- When adding or removing team members
- When reassigning team leads
- When reviewing team composition

**What good looks like:**
- Every team has a clear lead
- Team members are correctly assigned
- Team descriptions are up-to-date

**Best practices:**
- Keep team sizes manageable (3-8 people ideal)
- Assign a single team lead per team
- Update team composition when people change roles

### WELLBEING

---

#### Team Wellness

**What it is:**
Tracks individual employee wellbeing: mental health indicators, energy levels, and support needs.

**Why it exists:**
Creates a structured way for employees to share how they're doing, and for managers to respond proactively.

**When to use it:**
- When you notice someone struggling
- During regular check-ins
- After a high-stress period
- When an employee signals they need support

**What good looks like:**
- Check-ins happen regularly
- "Struggling" statuses get immediate attention
- Support needed is specific and actionable

**Best practices:**
- Take "Struggling" seriously
- Follow up with specific support offers
- Maintain confidentiality
- Look for patterns (multiple "Okay" statuses = trending downward)

### INSIGHTS — Data & Analytics Boards

---

#### Metrics

**What it is:**
A read-only KPI dashboard showing organization-wide metrics: hiring velocity, onboarding completion, team health scores, action item rates, and shipping metrics.

**Why it exists:**
You can't improve what you can't measure. This board provides a single source of truth for organizational health metrics.

**When to use it:**
- Daily: Quick health check
- Weekly: Review trends
- Monthly: Deep dive into metrics
- When presenting to leadership

**What good looks like:**
- Metrics are moving in the right direction
- You can explain why any metric changed
- You take action when metrics decline

**Best practices:**
- Don't just look at numbers — understand the story behind them
- Share relevant metrics with your team
- Use metrics to identify problems, not just to report
- Compare metrics over time

---

#### Reports

**What it is:**
A deep analytics engine. Generate cross-tabulated reports, trend analysis, and exportable data across all boards.

**Why it exists:**
Metrics tell you "what." Reports tell you "why." This board lets you slice and dice data to find root causes and make data-driven decisions.

**When to use it:**
- When investigating a metric change
- When preparing for leadership reviews
- When identifying hiring or retention patterns
- When benchmarking team performance

**What good looks like:**
- Reports are generated with specific questions in mind
- Filters are applied correctly
- Insights are actionable
- Reports are shared with relevant stakeholders

**Best practices:**
- Start with a question ("Why is turnover up?")
- Use filters to narrow the data
- Look for correlations, not just causation
- Act on insights — reports without action are wasted effort

## 4. Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Can't see a board | Check your role permissions; contact admin |
| Candidate not appearing | Verify they were added to Hiring Pipeline |
| Onboarding checklist missing | System generates on first save; re-save the card |
| Report shows no data | Check date range filters; verify data exists |
| Can't edit a record | Some records may be admin-only; contact admin |
| Data not updating | Check your internet connection; data syncs every 3 seconds |

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I delete a candidate from the pipeline?**
A: Yes, open the candidate card and click Delete. This action cannot be undone.

**Q: Can I change someone's role?**
A: Only admins can change user roles. Contact your admin.

**Q: How do I add a new team?**
A: Go to Teams > Create Team. You may need admin permission for this.

**Q: Can I see employee salary information?**
A: Compensation data is visible in My Profile (own data only) and Org Chart (admin/HR view).

**Q: How often does data sync?**
A: Data syncs every 3 seconds when the browser tab is active.
