## 1. Getting Started

### Login

1. Navigate to the HROS application URL
2. Enter your email and password
3. You'll land on **My Dashboard**

### Your Access Overview

As an Employee, you have access to **12 boards**:

| Category | Your Access |
|----------|-------------|
| MY SPACE | My Dashboard, My Profile, Organization, Work Uploads |
| TEAM | Team Sync, Team Work, 1:1 Meetings, Action Items |
| WELLBEING | Team Wellness |
| INSIGHTS | Metrics (read-only) |

### What You Cannot Do

- Access Hiring Pipeline
- Access Onboarding
- Access Internships
- Access Reports
- Access Admin Settings, System Logs, Commands

### MY SPACE — Personal Boards

---

#### My Dashboard

**What it is:**
Your personal command center. The first screen you see after login. It aggregates everything that needs your attention into a single, scannable view.

**Why it exists:**
Instead of checking multiple boards every morning, this surfaces your tasks, upcoming meetings, and team activity in one place. It helps you prioritize your day without context-switching.

**When to use it:**
- Every morning when you start work
- After returning from a break
- When you need a quick status check
- At the end of the day to log work

**What good looks like:**
- You open HROS, glance at the dashboard, and immediately know what needs attention
- No pending tasks are overdue
- You use Quick Action buttons for common tasks

**Quick Actions available to you:**

| Button | What It Does |
|--------|-------------|
| **Request Meeting** | Schedule a 1:1 with your manager |
| **Log Work** | Log your work for the day |
| **Request Time Off** | Submit a time off request |
| **Add Task** | Create a new task for yourself |

**Best practices:**
- Check it first thing every morning
- Use Quick Actions instead of navigating multiple boards
- Don't ignore the "Pending Tasks" section

#### My Profile

**What it is:**
Your personal identity card within HROS. It stores your name, email, role, team, skills, preferences, and compensation history.

**Why it exists:**
Centralizes your professional identity so other boards can reference it. When you're assigned to a project or team, your profile data flows into org charts and team views automatically.

**When to use it:**
- When you first join the organization (set up your profile)
- When you gain new skills or certifications
- When you want to review your compensation history
- When HR requests updated information

**What good looks like:**
- Complete profile with all fields filled in
- Skills list updated regularly as you learn new things
- Preferences reflecting your current work style

**What you can edit:**

| Field | Editable? |
|-------|-----------|
| Name | Contact admin to change |
| Email | Contact admin to change |
| Role | Contact admin to change |
| Skills | Yes |
| Preferences | Yes |
| Certifications | Yes |
| Bio | Yes |

**Best practices:**
- Keep your skills list current — it helps with project assignments
- Review compensation history before salary discussions
- Update preferences if your work situation changes

#### Organization (Org Chart)

**What it is:**
A visual map of your entire company's reporting structure. Shows who reports to whom, team compositions, and role distributions.

**Why it exists:**
Provides transparency. You can see the full hierarchy, understand reporting lines, and find the right person for cross-functional work. It builds trust through visibility.

**When to use it:**
- When you need to find someone's manager
- When you want to understand the team structure
- When you're looking for the right person to contact
- When you're curious about the organization

**What good looks like:**
- Every person is visible and correctly placed
- You can navigate the hierarchy easily
- Search works effectively

**How to navigate:**
1. **Zoom** — Scroll to zoom in/out
2. **Pan** — Click and drag to move around
3. **Click** — Click a person to see their name and role
4. **Search** — Use the search bar to find specific people

**Best practices:**
- Use the search bar to quickly find specific people
- Zoom out to see the big picture, zoom in for details
- Click on people to learn about their roles

#### Work Uploads

**What it is:**
A centralized workspace for sharing your deliverables with your manager and team. Creates a structured review workflow: Uploaded → Under Review → Approved/Needs Revision/Rejected.

**Why it exists:**
Replaces scattered file shares and email attachments. When you upload work here, it goes through a formal review process. You get feedback, ratings, and a clear status — not just a "looks good" in Slack.

**When to use it:**
- When you've completed a deliverable that needs review
- When you want to submit work for approval
- When you want to showcase finished projects
- When you need feedback on your work

**What good looks like:**
- Clear titles that describe the work
- Detailed descriptions explaining context and purpose
- Correct categorization (Document, Code, Design, Presentation)
- Complete, polished work (not drafts)

**Creating an upload:**
1. Click **"+ Add Upload"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Title** (required) — Name of your work
   - **Description** — What it is and why it matters
   - **Category** — Document, Code, Design, or Presentation
   - **File/Data** — Attach your file or content
   - **Tags** — Keywords for search
3. Click **Save**

**What happens after upload:**
1. Your manager reviews your submission
2. They add feedback and a rating
3. Status changes to Approved/Needs Revision/Rejected
4. You're notified of the decision

**Responding to feedback:**

| Status | What to Do |
|--------|-----------|
| **Approved** | Great work! Upload is complete |
| **Needs Revision** | Read feedback, make changes, upload revised version |
| **Rejected** | Read rejection reason, discuss with manager, try again |

**Best practices:**
- Upload finished work, not drafts
- Include a summary in the description
- Use the correct category for searchability
- Respond to "Needs Revision" feedback promptly
- Ask questions if you're unsure about feedback

### TEAM — Collaboration Boards

---

#### Team Sync

**What it is:**
A real-time standup replacement. Instead of scheduling a daily meeting, you post what you're working on, your status, and when you last updated.

**Why it exists:**
Daily standups are hard to schedule across time zones and busy calendars. Team Sync gives you the same information asynchronously. It surfaces blockers instantly so your manager can help before they become crises.

**When to use it:**
- Start of day: Post what you plan to work on
- Mid-day: Update progress if significant
- End of day: Post what you accomplished
- Immediately: If you hit a blocker

**What good looks like:**
- You update at least once daily
- "Blocked" statuses are resolved within hours
- Notes provide enough context without follow-up questions
- You can scan the board in 30 seconds

**Adding your update:**
1. Click **"+ Add Update"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Working On** — What you're currently focused on
   - **Status** — On Track / Blocked / Needs Help
   - **Notes** — Additional context
3. Click **Save**

**Status guide:**

| Status | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|
| **On Track** | Everything is going as planned |
| **Blocked** | Something is preventing your progress |
| **Needs Help** | You need assistance from someone |

**Responding to teammates:**
- **See "Blocked"?** — Offer help if you can assist
- **See "Needs Help"?** — Reach out to lend a hand
- **See "On Track"?** — Acknowledge with a reaction

**Best practices:**
- Update at least once daily
- Be specific in "Working On" — "reviewing PR #42" is better than "coding"
- Change "Blocked" to "On Track" immediately when unblocked
- Check for teammates' blocked statuses and offer help
- Use notes to provide context

#### Team Work (Kanban)

**What it is:**
A visual task management board using the Kanban methodology. Tasks move through columns: To Do → In Progress → Done.

**Why it exists:**
Provides a shared, visual representation of all work. Everyone can see what's being worked on, what's queued, and what's finished. It prevents work from falling through the cracks.

**When to use it:**
- When you start working on something (drag to In Progress)
- When you finish something (drag to Done)
- When your manager assigns you new work (it appears in To Do)
- When you need to check what's on your plate

**What good looks like:**
- Tasks are small and focused (can be completed in 1-3 days)
- "In Progress" column isn't overloaded
- "Done" column gets cleared regularly
- Each task has clear completion criteria

**Creating a task (for yourself):**
1. Click **"+ Add Task"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Title** (required) — Task name
   - **Description** — What needs to be done
   - **Priority** — Low / Medium / High / Critical
   - **Due Date** — When it's needed
3. Click **Save**

**Managing your tasks:**

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| Start a task | Drag from To Do → In Progress |
| Complete a task | Drag from In Progress → Done |
| Blocked | Keep in In Progress, add blocker details, update Team Sync |

**Task priority guide:**

| Priority | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| **Critical** | Do this first — blocking others or time-sensitive |
| **High** | Important — do soon |
| **Medium** | Regular work — do this sprint |
| **Low** | Nice to have — do when you have time |

**Best practices:**
- Keep tasks small — break large work into smaller pieces
- Update task status daily
- Don't leave tasks in "In Progress" for more than 3 days
- Add notes when something changes
- Use priority levels consistently

#### 1:1 Meetings

**What it is:**
A structured meeting scheduler and agenda manager for one-on-one meetings with your manager.

**Why it exists:**
1:1 meetings are where real conversations happen — about your work, your growth, and your wellbeing. This board ensures they're well-prepared and produce actionable outcomes.

**When to use it:**
- Before a meeting: Add prep notes and topics
- During the meeting: Listen, discuss, take notes
- After the meeting: Review summary and action items
- Between meetings: Follow up on commitments

**What good looks like:**
- Prep notes are written before the meeting (not improvised)
- Action items have clear commitments
- Meeting summaries capture key decisions
- Follow-up items are tracked to completion

**Preparing for your 1:1:**
1. Open the 1:1 card
2. Add **Prep Notes** — What you want to discuss:
   - Progress on current projects
   - Blockers or challenges
   - Career goals and development
   - Feedback (give and receive)
   - Workload concerns
   - Learning opportunities
3. Review your recent work uploads and tasks
4. Think about questions you want to ask

**During the meeting:**
1. Open the 1:1 card
2. Go through your prep notes
3. Listen to feedback
4. Take notes on action items
5. Ask clarifying questions

**After the meeting:**
1. Review the **Summary** — Key takeaways
2. Check **Action Items** — What you agreed to do
3. Follow through on commitments
4. Note any follow-up items for next time

**Good 1:1 topics:**
- Progress on current projects
- Blockers or challenges
- Career goals and development
- Feedback (give and receive)
- Workload concerns
- Team dynamics
- Learning opportunities
- What's working and what isn't

**Best practices:**
- Come prepared — don't improvise
- Be honest about challenges
- Follow up on previous action items
- Use the meeting for growth, not just status updates
- Don't cancel unless absolutely necessary

#### Action Items

**What it is:**
A decision-tracking and accountability board. Shows tasks and decisions assigned to you from meetings, 1:1s, and other sources.

**Why it exists:**
Decisions without owners don't get done. This board makes your commitments visible and trackable. It answers: "What did I agree to do, and when is it due?"

**When to use it:**
- When your manager assigns you an action item
- After meetings where you volunteered for something
- When you need to track a commitment

**What good looks like:**
- Every action item has a clear owner (you)
- Due dates are realistic
- Status is updated regularly
- Completed items are cleared

**Action item status:**

```
Assigned → In Progress → Review → Complete
```

| Status | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Assigned** | Given to you, not yet started |
| **In Progress** | You're working on it |
| **Review** | Done, waiting for verification |
| **Complete** | Finished and verified |

**Updating an action item:**
1. Open the action item card
2. Change the **Status** as you progress
3. Add notes about what you did
4. Update the **Completion %**

**Completing an action item:**
1. Finish the work
2. Change status to **Review**
3. Your manager will verify and mark as **Complete**

**Best practices:**
- Check your action items daily
- Update status regularly
- Don't let items sit in "In Progress" for too long
- Communicate if you're blocked or need more time
- Complete items by the due date

### WELLBEING

---

#### Team Wellness

**What it is:**
A safe space to track your mental health and wellbeing. Share how you're doing so your manager can support you.

**Why it exists:**
Burnout is expensive — for you and the organization. This board creates a structured way to signal when you need help, before things become crises. It's confidential and designed to help, not judge.

**When to use it:**
- When you're feeling overwhelmed
- When you need support
- When you want to share how you're doing
- After a high-stress period

**What good looks like:**
- You check in regularly (not just when things are bad)
- Your status honestly reflects how you feel
- "Support Needed" is specific and actionable
- You follow up on support offered

**Recording a check-in:**
1. Click **"+ Add Check-in"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Mental Health** — Good / Okay / Struggling
   - **Energy Level** — 1-5
   - **Support Needed** — What you need
   - **Notes** — How you're feeling
3. Click **Save**

**What each level means:**

| Level | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| **Good** | You're feeling positive and productive |
| **Okay** | You're managing but could use some support |
| **Struggling** | You need help — reach out to your manager |

**Confidentiality:**

Your wellness data is visible to:
- You
- Your direct manager
- HR (for pattern identification)

It is NOT visible to:
- Other team members
- Other managers

**Best practices:**
- Check in regularly, not just when things are bad
- Be honest — hiding problems doesn't fix them
- Be specific in "Support Needed" — "I need help with prioritization" is better than "I need help"
- If you're struggling, reach out — your manager wants to help
- Update your status when things change

### INSIGHTS

#### Metrics

**What it is:**
A read-only KPI dashboard showing organization-wide metrics: hiring velocity, onboarding completion, team health scores, and action item rates.

**Why it exists:**
Provides transparency into organizational health. You can see how the company is doing overall, which builds trust and context for your own work.

**When to use it:**
- When you're curious about company metrics
- When you want context for team discussions
- When preparing for all-hands meetings

**What you can see:**

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Hiring Velocity | How fast candidates move through pipeline |
| Onboarding Completion | % of new hires completing onboarding |
| Team Health | Aggregate team sentiment scores |
| Action Item Rate | Completion rate of action items |

**What you cannot see:**
- Individual performance metrics
- Manager effectiveness ratings
- Detailed reports (HR/TeamLead only)
- Salary or compensation data

**Best practices:**
- Don't just look at numbers — understand the story
- Use metrics to inform your conversations
- Compare over time (this month vs. last month)

## 3. Keyboard Shortcuts

| Shortcut | Action |
|----------|--------|
| `Ctrl/ Cmd + K` | Global Search — search across all boards |
| `Ctrl/ Cmd + /` | Command Palette — quick action access |
| `1-9` | Quick board switch (by sidebar order) |
| `Escape` | Close panels/modals |

**Using Global Search (Ctrl+K):**
1. Press **Ctrl+K** (or **Cmd+K** on Mac)
2. Type what you're looking for
3. See results from all boards
4. Click to navigate directly

**Using Command Palette (Ctrl+/):**
1. Press **Ctrl+/**
2. Type a command name
3. See matching actions
4. Press Enter to execute

## 4. Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Can't see a board | Check your role permissions; contact your manager |
| Can't edit a task | Verify you're the assignee |
| Can't upload work | Check file size and format |
| 1:1 not showing | Verify the meeting was scheduled by your manager |
| Data not updating | Check your internet connection; data syncs every 3 seconds |

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I see other employees' profiles?**
A: You can see basic info (name, role, team) on the org chart. Detailed profiles are restricted.

**Q: Can I create tasks for other people?**
A: You can only create tasks assigned to yourself. Your manager creates tasks for you.

**Q: Can I view the hiring pipeline?**
A: No, that's restricted to HR and admin roles.

**Q: Can I see team metrics?**
A: You can view high-level org metrics. Detailed team metrics are for managers and HR.

**Q: How do I update my skills?**
A: Go to My Profile > Edit Profile > Add or update your skills.

**Q: Can I request time off?**
A: Yes, use the Quick Action button on your dashboard.

**Q: What if I need to change my role?**
A: Contact your manager or HR to discuss role changes.

**Q: How do I contact HR?**
A: Use the 1:1 meeting scheduler or reach out via your team's communication channel.
